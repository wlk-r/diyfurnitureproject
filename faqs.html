Frequently Asked Questions

What do I get when I purchase a plan?

Each purchase includes detailed 3D models (viewable online and downloadable), complete cut lists, assembly instructions, hardware specifications, and finishing recommendations. You'll have lifetime access to the files and any future updates.

What skill level do I need?

Most designs are suitable for beginners with basic woodworking knowledge. If you can make accurate measurements, straight cuts, and follow assembly instructions, you can build these projects. Some intermediate skills like using a router or creating pocket holes may be needed for certain designs.

Can I modify the designs?

Absolutely. The plans are designed to be adaptable. You can adjust dimensions to fit your space, substitute materials, or modify details to suit your preferences. The 3D models help visualize changes before cutting any material.

Where can I source materials?

Most materials are available at home improvement stores like Home Depot or Lowe's. We primarily use plywood, which comes in standard sheet sizes. Hardware and fasteners are also readily available. Some specialty items or 3D printed components may need to be ordered online.

Do I need a 3D printer?

No. While some designs incorporate 3D printed hardware for convenience or improved functionality, they can be built without these components using standard hardware alternatives. Pre-printed parts may also be available for purchase.

How long does it take to build a project?

Build time varies by project complexity and your experience level. A simple shelf might take a weekend, while a bed frame could take a week of evening work. The plans help you organize the build into manageable phases.

Can I sell furniture I build from these plans?

You may build and sell physical furniture pieces for personal profit. However, you may not redistribute, resell, or share the digital plans, models, or instructions.

What if I need help during my build?

Contact us via email with photos or questions about your specific build. We're here to help troubleshoot issues and provide guidance to ensure your project succeeds.
